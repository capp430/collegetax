Tax Terms Explained

W-2 Form

A wage statement provided by your employer showing total earnings and taxes withheld during the year. Required to file federal returns.

Standard Deduction

A fixed amount subtracted from income before tax is calculated. Most college students use this instead of itemizing deductions.

Self-Employment Income

Income from freelance, gig, or contract work. Subject to self-employment tax (15.3%).

States With No Income Tax

Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Texas, Washington, Wyoming, Tennessee, New Hampshire*

*Some states tax interest/dividends only.

If you live or work in a state with income tax, you may need to file both federal and state returns. State tax rates and deductions vary.

How to File

You can file using IRS Free File or platforms such as TurboTax, H&R Block, Cash App Taxes, or TaxAct.

If you earned under IRS free-file thresholds, you may qualify for completely free filing.
