Tax Glossary

Filing Status

Your tax category (Single, Head of Household, etc.) which determines your standard deduction and tax bracket thresholds.

Standard Deduction

A fixed amount of income excluded from taxation. Most students use this instead of itemizing deductions.

Taxable Income

Your income after deductions are subtracted. This is what the IRS applies tax brackets to.

Withholding

Federal income tax already taken from your paycheck by your employer throughout the year.

Self-Employment Income

Income earned through freelance or contract work. Subject to self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare).

Marginal Tax Rate

The rate applied only to the last dollar earned — not your entire income.

How to Obtain Required Documents

• W-2 from employer (by January 31) • 1099-NEC for contract income • 1098-T from your college • Prior year return (if available)

How to File Your Taxes

You can file directly through the IRS Free File program or use trusted platforms such as:

  • IRS Free File (free for eligible income levels)
  • TurboTax
  • H&R Block
  • Cash App Taxes
  • TaxAct

Most students with simple W-2 income can file in under 30 minutes. If you have self-employment income, ensure you include Schedule C and Schedule SE.
