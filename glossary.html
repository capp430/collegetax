Tax Glossary & Filing Guide

W-2 Form

A W-2 is issued by your employer and reports total wages earned and federal/state taxes withheld during the year.

Standard Deduction

A fixed IRS amount that reduces taxable income before tax is calculated.

Taxable Income

The amount remaining after deductions that is actually taxed.

States With No Income Tax

Texas, Florida, Nevada, Washington, Wyoming, South Dakota, Alaska.

How To File Taxes

Gather your W-2, any 1099 forms, and your Social Security number. Choose a filing platform such as IRS Free File, TurboTax, FreeTaxUSA, or H&R Block. Enter income information carefully, review deductions, and submit electronically. You may receive a refund if you overpaid or owe if insufficient tax was withheld.
