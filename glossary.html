Tax Glossary & Filing Guide

W-2 Form

A W-2 is provided by your employer and shows total wages earned and federal/state taxes withheld during the year.

Standard Deduction

A fixed amount the IRS allows you to subtract from income before calculating tax.

Taxable Income

The amount remaining after deductions that is subject to federal income tax.

Marginal Tax Rate

The rate applied only to the last dollar earned, not your entire income.

Self-Employment Tax

A 15.3% tax covering Social Security and Medicare for freelance income.

States With No Income Tax

Texas, Florida, Nevada, Washington, Wyoming, South Dakota, Alaska.

How To File Taxes

Gather your W-2 and any 1099 forms. Choose a platform such as IRS Free File, TurboTax, FreeTaxUSA, or H&R Block. Enter income and withholding information carefully. Review whether you are owed a refund or owe tax. Submit electronically and track your refund if applicable.
